Dasymetric Map for Estimating Spatial Distribution of Children

Dec 17, 2018 in Data Analysis

Techniques used: Dasymetric mapping, GWR
Tools: ArcGIS 1.2, R

To estimate and visualize a distribution of infants in 2009, Seoul, Korea, this dasymetric map was made by using GWR (Geographically Weighted Regression) and spatial interpolation techniques.

The results in the map I indicate that there are more infants from pale yellow to dark red in a grid of 200m x 200m. The map becomes fundamental information to calculate equity measure by comparison with the calculated index of accessibility.

Reference
Kim, Kyusik, 2016, A Study on Spatial Equity of Opportunities to Use Public Childcare Services Focusing on National/Public Childcare Centers in Seoul, Seoul studies 17(1): 45-64.
