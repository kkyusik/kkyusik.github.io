Mapping Spatial Mismatch between Opportunity and Population

Posted on 2018-12-17 | In Data Analysis

Techniques used: Bivariate LISA (Local Indicators of Spatial Association)
Tools: ArcGIS 10.2, GeoDA

To examine spatial mismatch between childcare opportunity and children population, bivariate LISA was used. The map was visualized by using ArcGIS and estimated by using GeoDa.

The map suggests that darker purple grids mean that they have low in opportunity and high in children population. Therefore, the darker purple grids show that they are the grids which need more childcare opportunities.

Reference
Kyusik Kim, 2015, A Study on an Assessment of Spatial Equity about Opportunities to use Public Childcare Services: Focused on National/Public Childcare Facilities in Seoul, A Thesis of Master degree, Seoul National University.
